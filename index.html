Impact of Bad Habits on COVID-19
Some of the bad habits we have in our daily lives can actually play a role in our chances of getting COVID-19. In this data visualization, we are going to learn about the impact of bad living habits on COVID-19, such as excessive drinking, insufficient sleep, obesity and smoking etc.
Population Density of each County in US

The choropleth map shows the population density of each county in the United States by means of gradient color. We can see that places with more(denser) population have darker colors, and vice versa, places with lighter colors have fewer people.

By moving the mouse over a particular county, that county will be highlighted and local information will be displayed, making it easier for the user to view each county.

The Covid 19 Cases in US in 2020

The dot map shows all the COVID-19 case spread in the United States, and we can clearly see that the eastern United States has a much higher concentration of COVID-19 cases than the western United States.

At the same time, we can compare this map with the previous one, and we can see that the densely populated areas are the ones with the highest incidence of COVID-19 cases.

Unhealthy Life Habit Sum in 10 States

Poor living habits also play a role in the susceptibility to COVID-19: obesity, excessive drinking, lack of sleep and smoking are the most common bad habits in modern times.

In Texas, there are a lot of people with these bad habits, which has contributed to the high number of COVID-19 cases in Texas.

In South Carolina, relatively few people have these bad habits, which also leads to a much lower number of COVID-19 cases in South Carolina compared to Texas.

Drinking/Overdose and Covid Death in 10 States

Purple represents alcohol deaths, and pink represents COVID-19 deaths.

People who drink heavily also tend to be more susceptible to COVID-19.

Among the 10 states, we can find that states with a higher number of deaths due to alcoholism tend to have a higher number of deaths due to covid.

Green represents overdose deaths, and pink represents COVID-19 deaths.

People who use drug overdose also tend to be more susceptible to COVID-19.

Among the 10 states, we can find that states with a higher number of deaths due to drug overdose tend to have a higher number of deaths due to covid.

People Vaccinated or Death in 10 States

This lollipop chart shows the top 10 states with the most deaths due to COVID-19.

We can see that Califonia and Florida have a high number of deaths, while Georgia has a relatively low number of deaths, even only half of Califonia.

This lollipop chart shows the number of vaccinations in those 10 states.

We can see that Califonia and Florida, which have high death toll, have very low numbers of vaccination, whereas Georgia has high number of vaccination, which is why its death toll due to COVID-19 is low.

Life Expectancy VS Population in US

The donut chart on the left recorded the population of 10 states, while the scatter plot on the right recorded the proportion of people's life expectancy and vaccination rate in each state.
By clicking legend on the right of the scatter plot, we can see the proportions of people's life expectancy and vaccination rates in individual states.
